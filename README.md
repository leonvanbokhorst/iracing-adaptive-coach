## Week 07: The Click (Summit Point Main Circuit)

There's a moment when everything stops feeling hard. When the track that fought you yesterday suddenly feels like home. When the corner that was a lottery becomes a weapon.

Master Lonn found that moment at Summit Point.

He returned after seven months away, expecting rust. Instead, his first lap was 1.4 seconds faster than his old PB. The growth from Weeks 01-06 had compounded into something he couldn't see until he was back on familiar ground.

But that was just the beginning. The first session revealed two nemesis corners‚ÄîTurn 1 and the Carousel‚Äîaccounting for 88% of all rotation events. The gap to P4 (Shuning Gong) was 1.1 seconds. Not talent. *Commitment*.

Day two, seventeen minutes changed everything.

He walked into the session with two focus areas: T5 coasting (no trail braking into the Carousel) and T1 late braking (full commitment after the three cones). No chasing lap times. No heroics. Just deliberate, focused drilling.

The data came back: 59% oversteer reduction at T5. Automaticity forming at T1. PB matched without trying.

That evening, exhausted, he did a "flow session"‚Äîjust drive, have fun, see what stuck. Twenty-five minutes of rallycross-mode shenanigans. The result? **NEW PB: 1:15.550**. The morning's technique work had bedded in during play. Speed came as byproduct.

Day three brought the races. The morning was chaos‚Äîcontact at Lap 2, off-track at Lap 3, dropped from P1 to P8. He recovered to P2, setting his fastest lap on the final lap. Voice recording captured his mental journey: frustration ‚Üí acceptance ‚Üí focus ‚Üí confidence.

But the evening session was where it clicked.

Started on pole. Got passed by faster AI‚Äî*didn't fight it* (Meebewegen 2.0). Chased. Caught. Then, at T5, the corner he'd drilled two days earlier, he saw his moment. He carried speed through the apex, got better drive, and made a clean pass to take the lead.

The adaptive AI sped up. With two minutes left, the #8 car slipstreamed past and reclaimed P1.

Master Lonn's final lap? **1:15.800**. Only 0.25s off his practice PB. In a race. While chasing.

He finished P2. But the data told a different story: **10 out of 10 corners dialed**. Turn 1, which had been a 2.626-second lottery that morning, was now 0.087 seconds consistent. A 97% improvement in *one day*.

The voice recording proved what the telemetry couldn't see. "No trail braking," he said four times during the race. Carousel Entry brake point œÉ: 1.9 meters‚Äîthe same spot within two meters across ten laps of intense racing. Verbal self-cueing becoming automatic execution.

This wasn't luck. This was the result of focused practice ‚Üí flow consolidation ‚Üí race deployment. The learning cycle that Week 05-06 built, weaponized.

When the adaptive AI had to speed up to beat him, it wasn't a loss. It was validation.
