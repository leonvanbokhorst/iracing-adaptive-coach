#### Throttle: Why "Jerky" and What To Do

**The Fact**: Avg throttle jerk = 541.2 %/sÂ² 

**What This Means**: You're applying throttle in quick stabs rather than a smooth progressive squeeze. Think of it like a light switch (ON/OFF) vs a dimmer (gradual).

**Why It Happens**:
1. **Oversteer compensation** â€” When the rear feels loose, you instinctively lift quickly, then reapply quickly
2. **Corner exit anxiety** â€” Wanting to get on power ASAP instead of waiting for the car to settle
3. **Binary thinking** â€” "I'm either accelerating or I'm not" vs "I'm at 30%... 50%... 70%... 100%"

**The Impact**: 
- Unsettles the rear under power (weight transfer spike)
- Burns rear tires faster
- Loses traction on corner exit
- Makes the car harder to predict

**Actionable Advice**:

2. **Count to two** â€” From first throttle application to full throttle should take ~2 seconds on slow corners
