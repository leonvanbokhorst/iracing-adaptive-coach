**Some fast drivers** push slightly PAST the peak force feedback to extract the last bit of lateral force.

**How it works:**

## Part 5: Developing the Feel - Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: The Ramp Test

**Goal:** Learn to feel the force feedback ramp-up to peak

**How:**

1. Enter a constant-radius corner (e.g., a sweeper)
2. Hold constant speed
3. GRADUALLY add steering angle (slow ramp over 3-5 seconds)
4. Pay attention to force feedback as it builds
5. Note when it reaches PEAK (heaviest)
6. Note when it starts to DROP (past peak)

**What you'll learn:**
- The feeling of approaching optimal
- The moment you hit peak FFB
- The moment you go past it

**Repeat 10 times until you can feel the peak reliably.**

---

### Exercise 2: The Peak Hold Challenge

**Goal:** Hold optimal slip angle for as long as possible

**How:**

1. Enter a long corner (e.g., Turn 5 Winton, sweeping left)
2. Find the point where steering FFB is heaviest
3. Hold that EXACT steering angle and speed
4. Count how many seconds you can hold it

**Success criteria:**
- 3+ seconds of sustained peak FFB
- No corrections needed
- Smooth, stable

**This is the feeling of "at the limit."**

---

### Exercise 3: The Over/Under Drill

**Goal:** Learn the difference between under, optimal, and past optimal

**How:**

1. Lap 1: Take corner at 90% speed (under optimal) - note the feel
2. Lap 2: Take corner at 100% speed (optimal) - note the feel
3. Lap 3: Take corner at 105% speed (past optimal) - note the feel

**Compare:**
- Lap 1: Light steering, easy, no challenge
- Lap 2: Heavy steering, planted, singing tires
- Lap 3: Steering goes light, car pushes wide, squealing

**Repeat until you can identify which lap is optimal WITHOUT looking at lap times.**

## Part 6: The Feel in Different Cars

### Low-Downforce Cars (Ray FF1600, Miata)

**Feel:**
- Force feedback builds SLOWLY
- Peak is WIDE (easier to stay at optimal)
- Forgiving if you go slightly over
- Lots of time to feel and adjust

**Master Lonn's experience:** "The Ray gives me time. I can feel the peak building and react."

---

### High-Downforce Cars (Formula, GT3)

**Feel:**
- Force feedback builds QUICKLY
- Peak is NARROW (harder to stay at optimal)
- Punishing if you go over (immediate slide)
- Requires faster reactions

**Master Lonn's note:** "Haven't driven these much yet, but Little Wan says they're way less forgiving. The peak is sharp."

## Part 7: Common Mistakes & How to Fix Them

### Mistake #1: Ignoring Light Steering

**What happens:** Steering goes light, you add MORE steering to compensate

**Why it's wrong:** Light steering = you're PAST optimal. More steering = even MORE past optimal = even LESS grip

**Fix:** When steering goes light, REDUCE steering angle slightly, not increase it

---

### Mistake #2: Chasing the Perfect Lap

**What happens:** You push too hard trying to find the absolute limit every lap

**Why it's wrong:** Overshooting optimal (even by 1-2Â°) costs time AND consistency

**Fix:** Aim for 95-98% of optimal. Consistency > perfection

---

### Mistake #3: Relying Only on Sound

**What happens:** You judge slip angle by tire noise alone

**Why it's wrong:** Simulator sounds are often inaccurate

**Fix:** Use force feedback as PRIMARY sensor, sound as SECONDARY
