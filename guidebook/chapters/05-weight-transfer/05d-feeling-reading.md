## Part 1: Feeling Weight Transfer

### 1. Steering Weight

**What you feel:**

- **Heavy steering** = Front tires are loaded (gripping)
- **Light steering** = Front tires are unloaded (less grip)

**In the FF1600:**

- **Under braking:** Steering gets heavier (front loaded)
- **On throttle:** Steering gets lighter (front unloaded)
- **Mid-corner:** Constant steering weight = stable platform

**What it tells you:**

- If steering suddenly goes light mid-corner ‚Üí **front tires lost load** (too much throttle, or sudden brake release)
- If steering gets very heavy ‚Üí **front tires overloaded** (too much speed, or too much brake + turn)

### 2. Brake Pedal Feel

**What you feel:**

- **Firm, progressive resistance** = Good weight transfer, tires loaded
- **Sudden lockup** = Tire unloaded (too much rear bias, or weight shifted off rear)

**In the FF1600:**

- **Rear brake bias too high** = Inside rear locks instantly under trail braking
- **Front brake bias too high** = Front tires lock, can't turn

### 3. Throttle Response

**What you feel:**

- **Smooth acceleration** = Rear tires loaded and gripping
- **Wheelspin** = Rear tire(s) unloaded (inside wheel spinning)

**In the FF1600:**

- **Early throttle + tight turn** = Inside rear spins, no forward drive
- **Wait until car is straight** = Both rear tires loaded, strong acceleration

### 4. Seat-of-the-Pants (G-Forces)

**What you feel:**

- **Pushed back in seat** = Accelerating (rearward transfer)
- **Pushed forward (harness tension)** = Braking (forward transfer)
- **Pushed to the side** = Cornering (lateral transfer)

**In the FF1600:**

- The light weight means **transitions are sharp and instant**
- You can feel the exact moment weight shifts from brake to throttle
- Smooth transitions = smooth g-force changes = fast lap

### 5. Car Behavior

**What the car does:**

- **Nose dives under braking** = Forward weight transfer
- **Nose lifts on throttle** = Rearward weight transfer
- **Body roll in corner** = Lateral weight transfer
- **Rear kicks out** = Too much power on unloaded rear
- **Front pushes wide** = Front tires overloaded or underloaded

## Part 2: Reading Weight Transfer in Telemetry

Little Padawan can show you weight transfer in the data:

### 1. Brake Pressure + Steering Angle

**Trail braking signature:**

```
Brake Pressure:  |‚Äæ‚Äæ‚Äæ\___
Steering Angle:     /‚Äæ‚Äæ‚Äæ‚Äæ\___
                    ‚Üë
                 Turn-in point
```

**What to look for:**

- **Overlapping brake and steering** = Trail braking (combined transfer)
- **Brake releases before steering** = Straight-line braking only
- **Steep brake release** = Sudden weight transfer (risky)
- **Gradual brake release** = Smooth weight transfer (fast)

### 2. Lateral G-Force

**Lateral G tells you cornering load:**

- **Peak lateral G** = Maximum cornering force (lateral weight transfer)
- **Time at peak G** = How long you maintain cornering load
- **Oscillating G** = Unstable platform (too much steering correction)

**In the FF1600:**

- Typical peak lateral G: **1.0‚Äì1.3 G** (varies by corner)
- Smooth, consistent G-force curve = good weight management
- Spiky G-force curve = fighting the car

### 3. Longitudinal G-Force

**Longitudinal G tells you braking/acceleration:**

- **Negative G (deceleration)** = Braking (forward weight transfer)
- **Positive G (acceleration)** = Throttle (rearward weight transfer)

**The "combined G" moment:**

- **Lateral + Longitudinal G together** = Combined weight transfer
- **Smooth transition from negative to positive** = Good technique
- **Sudden zero-G moment** = Gap between brake and throttle (weight floats)

### 4. Speed Trace

**Minimum corner speed tells you weight transfer quality:**

- **Higher minimum speed** = Better weight transfer management
- **Speed drops sharply then recovers** = Too much braking into corner
- **Speed bleeds mid-corner** = Too early throttle (washed out front)

## The Feedback Loop

**The complete cycle:**

1. **Plan:** "I'll trail brake into Turn 5"
2. **Execute:** Drive the corner
3. **Feel:** Steering gets heavy (front loaded), harness pulls (braking), car rotates (rear light)
4. **Confirm:** Check telemetry - brake/steering overlap, smooth G-forces
5. **Improve:** Adjust technique based on feel + data

**This is how you master weight transfer.**
