# Chapter 11: Car Behavior - Understeer & Oversteer

**Understanding, identifying, and correcting the car when it exceeds the limit**

---

## Narrative Hook

Master Lonn is pushing hard through Turn 3 at Oschersleben. He's been getting faster all session, chipping away at his personal best. But this lap, something feels different.

The car enters the corner. He turns the wheel. And... nothing. The car keeps going straight. He adds more steering. Still nothing. The front tires are sliding, making an unhappy scrubbing sound, and the car plows wide toward the exit curb.

"UNDERSTEER! The front won't grip!"

Little Padawan watches the telemetry. "Master, let me ask you something. When the car started pushing, what did you do?"

"I turned MORE! I needed to make the corner!"

"And what happened?"

"It... got worse?"

"Exactly. Here's the thing about understeerâ€”and oversteer, and everything at 'the limit'â€”**your instincts are wrong**. What feels right makes it worse. What feels wrong makes it better. This chapter is about rewiring your brain."

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will:

- Understand the philosophy of "the limit" and what it truly means to drive fast
- Know why great drivers are faster (hint: they INCREASE available grip, not just use it better)
- Identify understeer through FEEL (force feedback) and VISUAL cues
- Correct understeer using the proper hierarchy of responses
- Identify oversteer through FEEL and VISUAL cues
- Correct oversteer and know when a spin is saveable vs. inevitable
- Apply recovery procedures in real-time without panic

## Key Principles Preview

Before diving into the sub-chapters, here are the core truths:

### On The Limit

> "Average drivers know how to use all of the available grip. **Great drivers know how to drive the car to INCREASE the amount of grip available.**"

### On Understeer Correction

> "Even when you are worried about running wide, you should still correct understeerâ€”because you will get MORE grip, MORE cornering, when you aren't understeering."

### On Instincts

> "Your natural instincts will make the car behavior WORSE, not better. Understeer? You'll add steering (wrong). Oversteer? You'll lift off (often wrong). This chapter teaches you the RIGHT responses."
